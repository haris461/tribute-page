The Greatest: Muhammad Ali

Muhammad Ali, born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr., is widely regarded as one of the most significant and celebrated sports figures of the 20th century. As a professional boxer, Ali's skill, charisma, and unyielding confidence captivated the world, both inside and outside the ring. His contributions to the sport of boxing are matched by his profound impact on civil rights and social justice, making him an enduring icon of courage and determination.

Beyond the ring, Ali's personal life was equally remarkable. He was known for his devout Islamic faith and his outspoken stance on various political and social issues. Ali's conversion to Islam and his refusal to be drafted into the Vietnam War on religious and ethical grounds were significant moments in his life. He faced considerable backlash for these decisions but remained steadfast in his beliefs. Ali's personal journey was marked by his resilience, faith, and a deep sense of justice, which continued to inspire millions around the world.
